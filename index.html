Crossing Over into

Lessons from Deuteronomy to the Fall of Jericho

Begin the journey ↓

I

Moses' Final Glimpse

After 40 years of wandering, Moses saw the land — but could not enter.

"The LORD showed him all the land… Then Moses the servant of the LORD died there in the land of Moab." — Deuteronomy 34:1–5

We can imagine the weight of that moment — Moses at the edge of the promise he had spent his life pursuing, handing the future to another.

II

A New Leader Rises

Joshua takes the mantle. A new chapter begins.

Moses read the Law one last time. Now Joshua must lead Israel forward into the promise their forefathers had only hoped to see.

III

Preparation for the Crossing

Consecration and obedience before the crossing. The people set their hearts fully on God. The priests carried the Ark into the Jordan first. Circumcision renewed the covenant — physically vulnerable, yet trusting God's protection over human strength. Spiritual readiness before receiving His promises.

"You shall set up great stones… and write upon them all the words of this law." — Deuteronomy 27:2–3

The water stopped only
after they stepped forward.

Faith before evidence.
Obedience before miracle.

IV

Commander of the Army of the Lord & The Battle Plan

Before Jericho, Joshua encountered a divine figure with a drawn sword. Joshua fell on his face in reverent awe, and God gave him an unusual battle plan — not by military might, but by faithful obedience.

"And it shall be, when you hear the sound of the trumpet, all the people shall shout with a great shout; and the wall of the city will fall down flat." — Joshua 6:5

V

Spies and Strategy

Joshua sends two spies — quietly, wisely, under God's guidance.

Unlike the 12 spies of Numbers 13–14, whose fear led to 40 years of wandering, this mission was born of faith, not fear.

"Go, view the land, especially Jericho." — Joshua 2:1

VI

The Walls of Jericho

March. Trust. Shout. The walls fall flat.

Six days of silent marching. On the seventh, they circled the city seven times and let out a great shout. What human strategy could not accomplish, God did.

Faith Obedience Courage Endurance Trust

VII

Rahab: Grace for the Faithful

An outsider's faith — woven into the lineage of Christ.

"The LORD your God, He is God in heaven above and on the earth beneath." — Joshua 2:11

Rahab appears in the genealogy of Jesus (Matthew 1:5) — a woman considered an outsider, woven forever into God's redemptive plan.

"Be strong and of good courage; do not be afraid, nor be dismayed, for the LORD your God is with you wherever you go." — Joshua 1:9

God calls us to step into His promises with faith, obedience, and courage — trusting His power rather than our own, even when the path seems uncertain or impossible.

Reflect

Discussion

Q1. What caused Israel to wander 40 years? Are there areas where we hesitate to trust God fully?

Q2. Can you think of situations that seemed "stuck" or impossible for a long time, but where God eventually brought change? How did that experience shape your faith?
